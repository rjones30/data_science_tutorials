help_outline How To Use This Website

This website presents a selection of challenge problems at varied levels of difficulty, together with the data you need to conduct your investigation.

  • Click on the links next to any of the individual tutorial activities listed below to download the zipped images containing the tutorial data and ipython (jupyter) notebook prototype.
  • Use a desktop tool to unpack the tutorial image into a directory on your local machine.
  • Choose whether you want to run the notebook inside the jupyter app on your local machine, or make use of an online jupterlab environment, eg. UConn Jupyterlab (requires a UConn netid to sign in) or Google collab.
  • Open the notebook in your chosen jupyter environment and follow the instructions to begin the tutorial. If you are using a remote jupyterlab service like Google collab, you will need to upload the contents of the tutorial image first.
  • Have fun with data!
ACTIVITIES
1. Decoding GPS signals

The GPS navigation system employs a constellation of several dozen satellites, all of which transmit continuous signals in the same frequency band. Like having to follow to a dozen simultaneous conversations going on at the same time in a crowded room, the receiver in your cell phone has to pick out and follow the transmission from each individual satellite. It does this using a technique known as "code division multiplexing" (more technically, CDMA) the same technology that is used by the cell phone network in North America. The input data for this tutorial activity consists of a sample digital stream covering several minutes from a GPS receiver. From this one signal, you will learn how to pick out a number of individual satellite signals, identify which satellites they are, and determine the time delay that measures the relative distance between the satellite and the receiver. You will know when you have succeeded when you are able to detect the unique hidden messages embedded in the signals from each satellite!

2. Finding the distance to individual stars

One of the landmark achievements of modern astronomy is the ability to measure the immense distances from the earth to individual light sources in the night sky. The key discovery (actually more than one) that enables accurate distances to be determined is the "standard candle" whose intrinsic brightness can be known apart from its apparent brightness in the night sky. A sequence of these standard candles at a succession of scales from nearby to far away is known as the cosmic distance ladder. The first standard candle on the distance ladder is the Cephied variable star, a special type of star whose brightness waxes and dims in an repeating pattern with a period closely related to its intrinsic brightness. The data in this tutorial consists of a table of many known Cephieds, giving both their apparent brightness in the night sky and the intrinsic brightness inferred from their period. Analysis of these data will allow you to discover the fact that the universe is expanding, and measure its present expansion rate known as the Hubble constant.

3. Identifying individual genes in a strand of DNA

A gene is a limited section of a longer strand of DNA located in the nucleus of the cell which carries the information needed to synthesize one specific protein. Synthesis of these proteins takes place within the body of the cell through a sequence of steps known as gene expression. Not every cell in a multicellular organism contains a complete set of these proteins because gene expression is regulated depending on the environment during cell division and growth. But all cells with a nucleus contain their own copies of the complete genetic code. This genome determines all of the heritable traits of an individual. To the extent that individual traits can be mapped to specific genes, it should be possible to come up with a comprehensive description of each individual's traits, including any that may not yet be expressed, by making a catalog of their genes and comparing them to a library of known genes from previously studied individuals. This lookup is complicated by the fact that slight differences often appear in the exact sequence of the same gene within different individuals, something known as an allele. The data in this tutorial consist of a reference library of known genes and a set of unknown alleles that you are tasked with identifying from the library. One of the challenges in this activity is common to many problems in data science, and that is finding expected patterns in a signal that contains a significant amount of noise.
